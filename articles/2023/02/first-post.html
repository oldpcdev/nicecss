First Post

This post serves as a “changelog” and to show you step-by-step how I built NiceCSS.

It took a lot of work to do it all.

Version 0.0.1.xy: Getting Started

I am ashamed of this version. In my head it seemed innovative, but in practice it was horrible and I had to rebuild it again, changing everything until I reached the BrownLooks theme.

Unfortunately, I didn't take screenshots. and I shall have to say right here what it looked like. The header was 30px high and 100px wide, was at the top of the screen, and had a light red to dark red gradient background. It had a banner with a bokeh background and the content was underneath it. It was terrible, you could barely see the sidebar.

Version 0.0.2.xy - 0.0.6.xy: Towards perfection

These versions were built at the last minute and I didn't sleep for a LONG time. But in the end, they were almost perfect.

The 0.0.2.xy and 0.0.3.xy were the ones that defined the design of the site. I drew everything in GIMP.

0.0.4 .xy- 0.0.6.xy Focused on details like the logo, icons and home page. Fixed several mini bugs. 0.0.5 was the version with the most sub-versions.

No screenshots too.

Version 0.0.7.xy - 0.0.8.xy: No new ideas

These versions aren't special and didn't bring almost anything new, just a redesign of the logo, the stylization of the posts. and fixed (many) bugs in CSS.

Screenshot of the 0.0.8 version

Version 0.0.9.xy - 0.1.0: Calm is the key to perfection

An (almost) happy ending for NiceCSS. What's new are bug fixes and the NiceCSS upload on GitHub Pages.

Thanks to instructure-ui for the icons and to element-plus by the umbrella of the logo. All fonts came from Google Fonts.
